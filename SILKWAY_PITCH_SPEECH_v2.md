## BACKUP: Q&A шпаргалка

**Q: Why is growth slow?**
Seasonality — summer is 2x winter. Plus we optimized unit economics first: CAC down 3x in 3 months.

**Q: Competitors?**
VOX (Russia) — Moscow only, ~$50K GMV/year. Dogsy — dogs only, Russia only. PetBacker — 5 sitters in Almaty. We're the only one with AI + cynologist + deep local ops.

**Q: What if Claude API gets expensive?**
Multi-model: Haiku for classification ($0.001), Sonnet for responses, Opus for deep analysis. Can switch providers in a day.

**Q: Privacy? 10K clients sharing a bot?**
Each client's data is siloed. Vector search filtered by user_id. Client A never sees Client B's data.

**Q: What about spam/abuse?**
Smart system: Haiku classifies relevance before Sonnet processes. Off-topic spam costs us $0.003 total. 6+ off-topic = silent block. Paying clients = unlimited.

**Q: How do you monetize care?**
We don't push. We seed: "Bobby has weight issues? Regular walks help — many clients see results in a month." Client comes back when ready. When we have a promotion matching their need — we reach out naturally.

**Q: Can this scale to US?**
The AI brain can. But Rover owns US. Our strategy: become Rover for CIS (53M pet owners, zero competition). The AI assistant as a separate product — that's the US play, through Family AI, not pet care.

**Q: Georgia — why?**
700K pets, zero competitors, friendly regulation. Test market before Uzbekistan.

**Q: What's the data moat exactly?**
139,238 real messages. 12,089 deals. 7,293 client conversations with context. Real fears, real emergencies — a puppy that got sick, an owner afraid to trust a stranger. This data can't be scraped or bought.
